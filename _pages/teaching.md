My teaching spans undergraduate and graduate levels across behavioral economics, environmental economics, and research methods. I have taught at universities in Colombia, the Netherlands, and Peru, and have experience designing courses that integrate field experiment methods with applied policy analysis.

---

## Graduate Courses

**Behavioral Economics: Environment and Development** *(Course Designer & Instructor)*
Undergraduate/Graduate · Universidad de los Andes · 2025

An applied introduction to behavioral economics with a focus on sustainability and development contexts. The course covers bounded rationality, social norms, nudges, and choice architecture, with an emphasis on how these mechanisms operate in environmental and poverty settings in developing countries. Students design and evaluate an original behavioral intervention as a semester-long project, applying experimental design and basic impact evaluation methods. The course integrates seminar-style discussion of frontier research with hands-on experimental exercises.

**Mixed Methods for Field Research in Social Sciences**
Master-level · Wageningen University & Research · 2023

A graduate methods course covering the design and implementation of field research combining quantitative and qualitative approaches, with applications in development and environmental contexts.

**Biodiversity Use and Conservation Systems**

**Economic Instruments for Environmental Policy**

## Undergraduate Courses

**Economics for Social Sciences** *(Tutorial Instructor)*
Bachelor-level · Tilburg University · 2025

Introductory micro and macroeconomics for first-year students. Tutorial sections covered supply and demand, externalities, public goods, and macroeconomic policy. Included a public goods experiment to test theoretical predictions with real behavioral data.

**Environmental Economics**
Undergraduate · Universidad Jorge Tadeo Lozano · 2014

A course covering the economic foundations of environmental policy, including externalities, common pool resources, valuation methods, cost-benefit analysis, and regulatory instruments. Used a mix of lectures, applied workshops, and a semester research project.

**Evaluation of Social Development Projects**
Bachelor-level · School of Environmental Sciences, Technological University of Pereira · 2011–2014
