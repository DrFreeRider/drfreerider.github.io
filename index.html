José D. López-Rivas

Associate Researcher, Universidad de los Andes · Bogotá, Colombia

Centro ODS

Universidad de los Andes

Bogotá, Colombia

I am an applied economist studying how social mechanisms shape the effectiveness of policy interventions in sustainability and development transitions. My research examines how information diffusion, peer effects, social norms, and reciprocity determine whether interventions — informational, regulatory, market-based, or financial — produce their intended effects in low-income settings.

Using field experiments, quasi-experimental methods, and discrete choice experiments across Colombia, Peru, Tanzania, and the Netherlands, I find that individual behavioral responses to policy are systematically conditioned by social context: who else is targeted, how information travels through communities, and whether interventions align with or cut against prevailing norms. These social dynamics operate across environmental, food system, and development domains, suggesting that accounting for them is a general condition for intervention effectiveness — not a context-specific nuance.

My work aims to improve the design of behavioral and development policy by identifying when and how social structure can be leveraged to amplify intended effects, and when it produces unintended ones.

  1. REE
    Spreading the word! Effects of a randomized normative informational campaign on residential water conservation
    Jos’\e D. L’øpez-Rivas
    Resource and Energy Economics, 2024
    Abs DOI HTML

    This paper reports the direct and spillover effects of a norm-based informational campaign on residential water usage. Using a two-stage randomized saturation design, I find targeted and spillover households reduced their water usage compared to the control group, with average reductions of 8.2% and 5.6%, respectively. The findings shed light on the efficacy of leveraging social influence and norms to foster environmentally responsible behaviors.

  2. Mar. Policy
    What is the economic value of coastal and marine ecosystem services? A systematic literature review
    Jose D. Lopez-Rivas and Juan Camilo Cardenas
    Marine Policy, 2024
    Abs DOI HTML

    This paper examines the economic value of marine and coastal ecosystem services by analyzing 67 studies published in scientific literature. The studies focus on ecosystem services provided by coral reefs, marine waters, and mangrove forests. Findings inform policy discussions about preserving these valuable ecosystems in the context of the climate crisis.

  3. WP
    Weathering conflict: the effect of resource shocks on livestock raids
    Nathaniel D. Jensen, Jose D. Lopez-Rivas, Karlijn Morsink, and 1 more author
    2025
    CSAE Working Paper
    Abs HTML

    We examine the impact of climate shocks and drought insurance on inter-communal pastoral conflict in Kenya and Ethiopia. Using NASA’s eMODIS satellite data and a 40-season longitudinal household dataset, we find that negative shocks increase conflict in dry seasons, while positive shocks fuel conflict in subsequent rainy seasons. We also find that while insurance reduces conflict on average, ex ante insurance coverage exacerbates conflict when pasture is abundant.

  4. WP
    El Niño is Coming: Assessing the Impact of Fine-Based Policy on Collective Action in Anticipation of a Drought in Colombia
    Jose D. Lopez-Rivas and D. León
    2024
    Working Paper
    Abs PDF

    This paper evaluates whether a fine-based regulatory policy activates collective action in water conservation when communities face an anticipated climate shock. Using quasi-experimental variation generated by the announcement of an El Niño event in Colombia, we examine how the threat of enforcement interacts with community-level cooperation incentives.
